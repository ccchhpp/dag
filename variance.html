about

Change, subtle or abrupt, is a reminder of how nothing is free from permanence and the transformation people and things go through are part of the evolving nature of the world. This is the focus of this project: to have a literal representation of the changes art is able to have in itself. In this series of illustrations, I have created interactive pieces that are capable of being stand-alone artworks. The viewer is an extension of the piece, and their interaction is what dictates how they are able to perceive the illustrations.

An augmented reality application can be downloaded and utilized in conjunction with the finished illustrations, and upon using it, will reveal an overlay onto the piece and display an animation that transforms the piece to view a different kind of perspective on the preexisting piece. The use of the augmented reality application is not necessary, but will depend on the viewer’s desire to use it or not.

I have chosen to illustrate imagery in my own aesthetic style, and upon overlay, will animate a change into another form of imagery that is still my own visual aesthetic but with a subject to juxtapose one another. My goal is to reveal the unseen, and augmented reality is what really allows for this concept to take form. The idea of masks, revealing what is under the skin is interesting to see as it puts a somewhat visual, tangibility to something unspoken and invisible.

purpose

The reason I want to do this is because I wanted to show a hybridization of what is considered “traditional” forms of art for consumption with the interactivity that audience members have come to see in modern day works that incorporate computing and art. Before any form of digital media came into contact with art, the subject usually only included stationary pieces, such as paintings and sculpture. I wanted to represent how the shift from traditional to digital does not mean choosing one or the other, but the ability to incorporate the two into a functioning piece. Part of the purpose of the piece is to show the evolution of one-dimensional pieces (in terms of what you could do with a piece of art) to a multi-dimensional piece that allows a viewer to see a painting depending on whether they decide to look at it unaided or through a screen.

I also chose to use augmented reality applications in order to show animations in a non-destructive way that would not alter the isting image. Typically, we only see animations displayed on a screen if we want to display it as a “moving painting.” But I want to take advantage of the different digital tools that allow for an animation to be seen without the painting being displayed on the screen itself.

Simply creating animations overlaying paintings is not my sole purpose in this project either. Augmented reality is able to pull back a curtain and showcase something with its technology, and the content should be able to reflect this subject matter as well. Much like the illustrations have an underlying imagery, individuals also carry a type of underlying part of themselves that stay hidden to the outside world. In the illustrations, I will be able to show what remains concealed by revealing them through the use of augmented reality.
